The HomeBrew Robotics Club was resurrected from the ashes of the original HomeBrew Computer Club by a group enthusiasts who were interested in the emerging field of robotics.

HBRC members have a range of expertise in robotics that includes beginners, experienced amateurs and professionals. There are no age restrictions, so if you have an interest in the field of robotics, please join us.

We currently meet from 7:00 pm to 10:00 pm on the last Wednesday of every month (except December).

The venue for the meeting is being hosted by Google (courtesy of Ryan Hickman) on their campus.

HBRC Info

HBRC MAIL LIST

If you are interested in robotics and would like to keep abreast of club and member news, you will want to join our mail list. If you have a Google Groups account, you can simply add our list to your personal groups. If not, it is easy to set up a free Google Groups account by visiting Google.com. Our mail list can be found at: http://groups.google.com/group/hbrobotics

HBRC SIG

The HBRC Special Interest Group (SIG) is where members help each other solve construction, software, and design problems incurred while building their personal robots.

This meeting is held from 7:00 to 10:00 PM. at the Hacker Dojo in Mt. View (599 Fairchild Dr.) on the 2nd to last Wednesday of the month i.e. one week before the General Meeting.

"DJI Onboard SDK"
by Arjun Menon of DJI Labs

The Onboard SDK is a software platform built on DJI APIs that allows you to talk to the aircraft. What is cool is that most of the Onboard SDK is Open sourced down to the protocol level. The Onboard SDK is supported mainly by two of our aircrafts - the Matrice 100 and the Matrice 600. The former has a 1Kg payload and the latter has a 6Kg payload. The cool part of this platform is that the Onboard SDK can run on a computer of your choice onboard the aircraft. This means you can have a Intel i5 powered NUC doing all your computer vision algorithms and directing the aircraft where to go. This means you can take an Nvidia TX1 and run all your algorithms on it.
